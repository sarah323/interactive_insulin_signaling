About Insulin Signaling Explorer

Purpose

Insulin Signaling Explorer is an interactive educational resource designed to facilitate understanding of insulin signaling and insulin resistance through dynamic visualization of key molecular pathways. Rather than presenting signaling as a static diagram, the simulator allows learners to explore pathway activation, identify critical signaling nodes, and compare normal physiology with insulin-resistant states in an intuitive interactive environment.

Learning Objectives

After completing this activity learners should be able to:

  • Explain insulin receptor signaling.
  • Describe the functions of the insulin receptor, IRS proteins, PI3K, AKT, mTOR, FOXO1 and GLUT4.
  • Distinguish between metabolic and mitogenic signaling pathways.
  • Explain how insulin resistance alters intracellular signaling.
  • Relate molecular signaling abnormalities to metabolic disease.
  • Integrate molecular signaling with physiological and clinical concepts.

Target Audience

  • Medical students
  • Biomedical Sciences students
  • Pharmacy students
  • Dentistry students
  • Clinical Biochemistry trainees
  • Graduate students
  • Medical educators

Educational Note

This simulator uses simplified relative activity values to illustrate biological concepts. The numerical values are conceptual and should not be interpreted as experimentally measured biological quantities. This simulator is intended solely for educational purposes.
